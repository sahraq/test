NYC Cultural Accessibility Initiative

Making NYC's Cultural Life Accessible to Everyone

CulturalAccessNYC is building the resources, community, and systemic change needed to achieve real disability equity across New York City's arts and cultural institutions — by 2030.

Our Focus

Where We Work

Disability equity in NYC's cultural sector spans many interconnected challenges. We focus where the gaps are greatest.

Education & Training

Building disability literacy and accessibility competency across arts organizations — from front-of-house staff to executive leadership.

Workforce Development

Expanding pathways for disabled artists, administrators, and technicians to build careers in NYC's cultural institutions.

Equipment & Services

Connecting organizations with assistive technology, interpretation services, captioning, and access tools they need to serve all audiences.

Core Concepts

Beyond Compliance

Legal compliance is the floor, not the ceiling. We work toward something more meaningful.

01

Compliance

Meeting minimum legal requirements under the ADA. Necessary, but rarely sufficient to create genuinely welcoming experiences.

02

Functional Access

Going beyond the letter of the law to ensure disabled people can actually use and participate in cultural programming.

03

Experiential Equity

The goal: disabled audiences, artists, and staff have experiences of equal quality and dignity to everyone else.

Equity & Justice

Disability Does Not Exist in Isolation

Disability intersects with race, class, gender, immigration status, and other identities in ways that compound inequity. Our work is grounded in an understanding of these intersections and a commitment to justice, not just access.

That means listening to disabled communities rather than designing for them, and centering the people most affected in every decision we make.

Who We Serve

Everyone in NYC's Cultural Ecosystem

Disability equity in the arts isn't just about audience members. It encompasses everyone who participates in NYC's cultural life.

Artists & Performers

Disabled artists deserve full access to residencies, commissions, stages, and studios — not just modified participation.

Audiences

Every New Yorker should be able to attend, enjoy, and fully participate in the city's cultural offerings — on equal terms.

Staff & Administrators

Cultural institutions should reflect the communities they serve — including in their hiring, promotion, and working conditions.

Organizations

From small community spaces to major institutions — we support organizations at every scale in building genuine accessibility.

Our Approach

How We Work

CulturalAccessNYC is organized around four mutually reinforcing strategies.

1

Convene

Bringing together the people doing this work — disability advocates, arts leaders, funders, and artists — to share knowledge and build community.

2

Resource

Developing and curating the guides, toolkits, data, and funding connections that organizations need to make progress on accessibility.

3

Invest

Directing funding toward the organizations and individuals doing the hardest work — prioritizing those who have historically been under-resourced.

4

Advocate

Pushing for systemic change at the institutional and policy level — because individual accessibility improvements aren't enough on their own.
