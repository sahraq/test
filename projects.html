Road to 2030

A Timeline for Change

2030 was chosen to coincide with the 40th anniversary of the Americans with Disabilities Act — a meaningful milestone, and a reasonable timeline for real, measurable progress across the sector.

Foundation

Steering committee formation, needs assessment across NYC cultural institutions, and identification of priority focus areas.

Complete
2025–2026

Infrastructure & Partnerships

Building the organizational infrastructure, forging partnerships with funders and institutions, and launching pilot programs in education and workforce development.

In Progress

Scale & Advocacy

Scaling successful programs, publishing findings and toolkits for the field, and engaging in policy advocacy at city and state levels.

Planned

Sector-wide assessment of progress, public reporting on institutional change, and celebration of the ADA's 40th anniversary — marking how far NYC's cultural sector has come.

Current Work

Active Initiatives

These projects are currently underway, with more in development.

Active

Accessibility Leadership Training

A cohort-based professional development program for accessibility staff at NYC cultural institutions — covering disability justice frameworks, practical tools, and peer exchange.

  • 12-week cohort format
  • Open to staff at NYC nonprofits
  • Led by steering committee members
Active

Small Venue Accessibility Fund

Pilot grant program providing small and mid-sized cultural venues with direct funding for physical accessibility improvements — ramps, accessible bathrooms, hearing loop systems, and more.

  • Grants up to $25,000
  • Priority for BIPOC-led organizations
  • Technical assistance included
Active

NYC Cultural Accessibility Index

A publicly available database rating accessibility across NYC's major cultural institutions — aggregating information on physical access, ASL interpretation, captioning, audio description, sensory-friendly programming, and more.

  • Self-reported + community verified
  • Updated annually
  • Free and publicly searchable
In Development

Disabled Artists in Residence

Partnering with NYC cultural institutions to create paid artist residency slots specifically for disabled artists — with venues committed to providing full access support throughout the residency.

  • 6–12 month residencies
  • Host institution network in formation
  • Launching 2027

Bring This Work to Your Organization

CulturalAccessNYC works with organizations at every stage of their accessibility journey — from initial assessment to long-term culture change. Here's what we offer.

Accessibility Audits

A structured review of your organization's physical access, digital presence, programming, hiring, and communications — with a prioritized action plan.

Staff Training

Custom workshops and ongoing training on disability awareness, accessible communications, and creating welcoming environments for disabled audiences and staff.

Strategic Planning Support

Facilitated planning sessions to help your leadership embed accessibility and disability equity into institutional strategy, values, and operations.
